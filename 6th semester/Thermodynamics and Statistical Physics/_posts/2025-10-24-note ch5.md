## 5.2 Free Energy as a "Force" toward Equilibrium.
  2nd law : $d S_{total} \ge 0 \quad$ (in an isolated system)

  $= "universe"$

  [Diagram: Universe box containing System box and Reservoir box]

  $d S_{total} = d S + d S_R$,  $R$: reservoir

  for System.
  $d S = \frac{1}{T} d U + \frac{P}{T} d V - \frac{\mu}{T} d N$.

  for reservoir
  $d S_R = \frac{1}{T_R} d U_R + \frac{P_R}{T_R} d V_R - \frac{\mu_R}{T_R} d N_R$.

  Under the condition where $T, V, N$ are fixed

  $\Rightarrow d V = 0 \quad d V_R = 0$
  
  $d N = 0 \quad d N_R = 0$

  $T_R = T$ very close to Thermal equilibrium.


  Only free variables : $d U = -d U_R$ 
  (Total Energy is conserved)

  $\therefore d S_{total} = d S + \frac{1}{T_R} d U_R = d S - \frac{1}{T} d U$

  moving 
  From the state near eqilibrium
  or just with $T = T_R, d V=0, d N=0$

  $d S_{total} = -\frac{1}{T} (d U - T d S) = -\frac{1}{T} d F$

  [Diagram: Potential well F minimum]

  $\boxed{\therefore d F \le 0 }$ 
  
  System moves toward
  F minimum !!!
  When T, V, N fixed.

* Thermodynamic identity for H:

  $d H = T d S + V d P \quad \equiv H(P, T)$

  $\Rightarrow d H = T (\frac{\partial S}{\partial T})_P d T + T (\frac{\partial S}{\partial P})_T d P + V d P$

  $(\frac{\partial S}{\partial P})_T = - (\frac{\partial V}{\partial T})_P$
