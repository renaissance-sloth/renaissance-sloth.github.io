ii) thermal - conducting wall (After waiting for "long" time)

Total multiplicity
$$\Omega(U) = \sum_{U_A} \Omega_A(U_A) \Omega_B(U - U_A) \quad$$
Total $U$
where $U = U_A + U_B = $ fixed (Isolated)

Prob. of A being in $U_A$:
$$P_A(U_A) = \frac{\Omega_A(U_A) \Omega_B(U-U_A)}{\Omega(U)} \quad$$

Q. What is the most probable $U_A$? $(\equiv U_A^*)$

$\rightarrow$ maximizing $P_A(U_A)$!

$\therefore \frac{\partial }{\partial U_A} P_A(U_A) = 0$ at $U_A = U_A^*$

$$\implies 0 = \frac{\partial \Omega_A(U_A)}{\partial U_A} |_{U = U_A^*}\Omega_B(U - U_A^*) + \Omega_A(U_A^*) \frac{\partial}{\partial U_A}\Omega_B(U - U_A^*)|_{U = U_A^*}$$

$$= \frac{\partial \Omega_A(U_A)}{\partial U_A}|_{U = U_A^*} \Omega_B(U_B^*) + \Omega_A(U_A^*) \cdot (-\frac{\partial }{\partial U_B}\Omega_B(U_B)|_{U_B=U_B^*}$$

$$\because U = U_A + U_B = const.$$

(*) $\implies \frac{1}{\Omega_A(U_A^*)} \frac{\partial \Omega_A(U_A)}{\partial U_A}|_{U_A^*} = \frac{1}{\Omega_B(U_B^*)} \frac{\partial \Omega_B(U_B)}{\partial U_B}|_{U_B^*} \quad$
$M(eqr.)$

(**) : In thermal equilibrium
$$T_A = T_B \quad$$
$$\implies \frac{\partial }{\partial U_A}\ln \Omega_A(U_A)|_{U_A^*} = \frac{\partial }{\partial U_B}\ln \Omega_B(U_B)|_{U_B^*}$$

(***) : $\Omega$ is highly related to the 2nd law.
$$S \propto \ln \Omega \quad$$

(****) unit of Entropy.
$$[S] = [\frac{\partial Q}{T}] \quad$$
(Clausius theorem)

A relation satisfying all of (* - ****)

## 3.1 **Temperature** !!!

some examples (Energy Equation of state)

i) Einstein Solid (Eq. 2.46)
$$S = N k_B [\ln \frac{q}{N} + 1] \quad \text{high-T limit} \quad (q \gg N) \quad$$
$$\implies T = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial U})^{-1} = (\frac{N k_B}{U})^{-1} \quad$$
$$U \propto q \quad$$
$$S = N k_B \ln U + const(N) \quad$$
$$\implies U = N k_B T \quad$$

ii) Ideal gas. (Eq. 2.48)
$$S = N k_B \ln V + N k_B \ln U^{3/2} + const(N) \quad$$
$$\implies T = (\frac{3}{2} N k_B / U)^{-1} \quad$$
$$\implies U = \frac{3}{2} N k_B T \quad$$

## 3.4. Mechanical Equilibrium and Pressure.
Microcanonical ensemble
- Again, two-subsystems coupled to each other.

(Diagram: Two coupled systems with movable wall)
$\rightarrow$ thermal-insulating.

$U, N$ fixed.

$\rightarrow$ BUT, MOVABLE!

$$\implies \text{maximize } P_A(V_A) \quad \| \quad P_A(V_A) = \frac{\Omega_A(V_A) \Omega_B(V-V_A)}{\Omega(V)} \quad$$
$$\frac{\partial P_A}{\partial V_A}\|_{V_A^*} = 0 \quad$$

$$\therefore \frac{\partial}{\partial V_A} \ln \Omega_A\|_{V_A^*} = \frac{\partial}{\partial V_B} \ln \Omega_B\|_{V_B^*} \quad$$
and we know that at equilibrium,
$$P_A = P_B \quad (\text{pressure}) \quad$$

$$\therefore \frac{P}{T} = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial V})_{U, N} \quad$$
$\leftarrow$ dimensional analysis, $$[S] = [\frac{E}{T}] \quad$$
$$[P V] = [E] \quad$$
$$\implies [\frac{P}{T}] = [\frac{S}{V}] \quad$$

---

check for an ideal gas
$$\Omega = f(N) V^N U^{3N/2} \quad$$
$$S = N k_B \ln V + \frac{3}{2} N k_B \ln U + k_B \ln f(N) \quad$$
$$\implies \frac{P}{T} = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial V})_{U, N} = \frac{N k_B}{V} \quad$$
$$\implies P V = N k_B T: \quad \text{pressure equation of state.} \quad$$

*   Thermodynamic identity.
$$S = S(U, V) \quad$$
for infinitesimal changes in $U, V$
$$d S = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial U})_V d U + (\frac{\partial S}{\partial V})_U d V \quad$$
$\uparrow \text{ exact differential} \quad$
$$d S = \frac{1}{T} d U + \frac{P}{T} d V \quad$$
$$\implies d U = T d S - P d V \quad (\text{Thermodynamic identity}) \quad$$

Q. BUT, What's the physical meaning of "infinitesimal" here?
$\rightarrow$ compare with 1st law
2nd law

---

1st law: dep. on paths.
$$d U = \partial Q + \partial W \quad$$
(Work done on the system)

2nd law: $$d S = \frac{\partial Q}{T} \quad (\text{reversible process}) \quad$$

$\therefore$ along a reversible process
$$d U = T d S + \partial W. \quad \rightarrow \text{ slow, all stages are at eq.} \quad$$
$\therefore$ along a quasistatic path
where $P$ is well defined and uniform.
$$\partial W = - P d V \quad$$
$$\implies d U = T d S - P d V. \quad$$
(rev. $\oplus$ quasistatic)

In general.
$$T d S \ge \partial Q = d U - \partial W \quad$$
$$= d U + P d V: \quad \text{quasistatic.} \quad$$
$\rightarrow =$ reversible

$$T d S = d U + P d V: \quad \text{"fundamental"} \quad$$
thermodynamic relation.
Remark: reversible $\implies$ quasistatic (거꾸로는 안됨)

---

ex.) Entropy production - ($T d S \ge d Q$)
Free expansion

at eq. (Diagram: partitioned isolated box)
isolated: $\partial Q = 0$

at eq. (Diagram: gas fills entire box)

## 3.5. Diffusive Equilibrium and Chemical Potential.
Again, microcanonical ensemble for two coupled systems.

(Diagram: partitioned isolated box, particles can penetrate)
Isolated. $U, V$ fixed
$N = N_A + N_B$
particle can penetrate

$$\implies \text{maximize } P_R(N_A) \quad \| P_A(N_A) = \frac{\Omega_A(N_A) \Omega_B(N-N_A)}{\Omega_{tot}} \quad$$
$$\frac{\partial P_A}{\partial N_A}\|_{N_A^*} = 0 \quad$$

$$\implies \frac{\partial}{\partial N_A} \ln \Omega_A\|_{N_A^*} = \frac{\partial}{\partial N_B} \ln \Omega_B\|_{N_B^*} \quad$$

Introduce a new derived quantity.
"Chemical potential"
$$\mu = - T(\frac{\partial S}{\partial N})_{U, V} \quad$$

---

Chemical equilibrium: $$\mu_A = \mu_B \quad$$
(diffusive)

Meaning: at very close to equilibrium, but not at eq.

$\frac{d P_A}{d t} \propto -\frac{1}{T} (\mu_A - \mu_B)$

if $\mu_A < \mu_B$: particles flows from B to A

$\mu_B > \mu_A$: particles flow from A to B.

$\therefore$ Chemical potential: potential of particle \#.
particles flows from high $\mu$ to low $\mu$.

for infinitesimal changes in $U, V, N$
$$S = S(U, V, N) \quad$$
$$d S = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial U})_{V, N} d U + (\frac{\partial S}{\partial V})_{U, N} d V + (\frac{\partial S}{\partial N})_{U, V} d N \quad$$
$$d S = \frac{1}{T} d U + \frac{P}{T} d V - \frac{\mu}{T} d N \quad$$
$$\implies d U = T d S - P d V + \mu d N. \quad$$

for multiple species.
$$\implies d U = T d S - P d V + \sum_i \mu_i d N_i. \quad$$

NOTE: In chemistry $\mu_{chem} = -T (\frac{\partial S}{\partial n})_{U, V} \quad n$: mole
$n = N/N_A$

## Summary

In the microcanonical Ensemble,
$$d U = T d S - P d V + \mu d N \quad (\text{along reversible processes}) \quad$$

| Macrostate | Derived Var. | definitions |
|---|---|---|
| $U$ | $T$ | $\frac{1}{T} = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial U})_{V, N}$ |
| $V$ | $P$ | $\frac{P}{T} = (\frac{\partial S}{\partial V})_{U, N}$ |
| $N$ | $\mu$ | $\frac{\mu}{T} = - (\frac{\partial S}{\partial N})_{U, V}$ |


$$S \equiv S(U, V, N) \quad$$
