# Chapter 2. The Second Law

* Central limit Theorem. (law of large numbers)

Suppose $\\{X_1, X_2, \dots \\}$ is a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with $E[X_i] = \mu$ and $Var[X_i] = \sigma^2 < \infty$.

as $n \to \infty$

$$\sqrt{n} (\bar{S}_n - \mu) \longrightarrow N(0, \sigma^2) \quad \left( \bar{S}_n = \frac{X_1 + \dots + X_n}{n} \right)$$

$$p(S) \propto \exp \left[ -\frac{(S - \mu)^2}{2 (\sigma^2 / \sqrt{n})^2} \right] \quad \text{see Wikipedia.}$$

(P(S)와 S 축의 종 모양 곡선 다이어그램, $\propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}$로 N이 증가함에 따라 더 날카로워짐을 표시함)

$$\Rightarrow \text{How is this relevant to physics ?}$$

## 2.1 Two - State Systems.

"microstate" vs. "macrostate"

by example with three coins: Penny, Nickel, Dime.

Now on, in this chapter and chapter 3

We only consider isolated

"Microcanonical ensemble" "An isolated system" !

(격리된 시스템 상자 다이어그램)

If the system is isolated (no external influence!)

Flipping coins gives $\xrightarrow{\text{equal w. probability "1/2"}}$ "Head" or "Tail"

"All possibilities" : $2^3 = 8$

| Penny | Nickel | Dime | | Heads |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---|:---:|
| H | H | H | $\leftarrow$ | 3 head |
| H | H | T | $\leftarrow$ | 2 |
| H | T | H | $\leftarrow$ | 2 |
| T | H | H | $\leftarrow$ | 2 |
| H | T | T | $\leftarrow$ | 1 |
| T | H | T | $\leftarrow$ | 1 |
| T | T | H | $\leftarrow$ | 1 |
| T | T | T | $\leftarrow$ | 0 |

* What is a "microstate" ?

$\xrightarrow{\text{ex}} \left\\{ H, T, H \right\\} :$ one possible configurations.

\# of microstates $= 2^3$

* What is a "macrostate" ? : depends on question.

for example, how many heads are there ?

$\Rightarrow$ \# of heads.

There is no one - to - one correspondence between micro- and macrostates !!!

* "Multiplicity" = \# of microstates giving the same macrostate.

Symbol $\Omega$

ex. $\Omega$ (3 heads) = 1, $\Omega$ (2 heads) = 3.

probability can be defined as

$$\text{prob. (n heads)} = \frac{\Omega(n)}{\Omega_{\text{total}}}$$

* generalization.

For N distinguishable coins,
\# of microstates giving n heads,

$$\Omega(N, n) = _N C_n \equiv \binom{N}{n}$$

$$= \frac{N!}{n! (N-n)!}$$

***

Another example: two-state paramagnet.

$\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow \uparrow \downarrow \uparrow \uparrow \downarrow \uparrow \downarrow \dots \quad (B=0)$

: spin up-down is random.

\# of microstates giving $N_{\uparrow}$ spins are up.

$$= \Omega (N_{\uparrow}) = \binom{N}{N_{\uparrow}} = \frac{N!}{N_{\uparrow}! N_{\downarrow}!} \quad (N_{\uparrow} + N_{\downarrow} = N)$$

## 2.4. Large systems.

(N, q small과 N, q large 시스템 비교 다이어그램: large 시스템이 훨씬 날카로운 피크를 가짐)

Einstein Solid

$$\Omega(N, q) = \binom{q + N - 1}{q} = \frac{(q+N-1)!}{q! (N-1)!}$$

$$= \frac{(q+N)!}{q! N!} \cdot \frac{N}{q+N}$$

$$q \gg 1, N \gg 1$$

$$\ln \Omega = \ln (q+N)! - \ln q! - \ln N! + \ln N - \ln (q+N)$$

$$\approx (q+N) \ln (q+N) - (q+N) - q \ln q + q - N \ln N + N$$

$$+ \ln N - \ln (q+N)$$

$$\rightarrow \text{very small compared with the others.}$$

***

$$\ln \Omega \approx (q+N) \ln (q+N) - q \ln q - N \ln N$$

Now, let's consider $q \gg N$ for simplicity (high-T limit)

$$\ln (q+N) = \ln \left[ q \left( 1 + \frac{N}{q} \right) \right]$$

$$= \ln q + \ln \left( 1 + \frac{N}{q} \right)$$

$$\approx \ln q + \frac{N}{q}$$

$$\therefore \ln \Omega \approx N \ln \frac{q}{N} + N + \frac{N^2}{q} \quad \text{small}$$

$$\Omega (N, q) \approx \left( \frac{e q}{N} \right)^N \quad (q \gg N)$$

In $\leftarrow$ intueity form. because...
(interacting large systems)
(A와 B 박스 다이어그램)

$$\Omega = \Omega_A \Omega_B$$

$$= \left( \frac{e q_A}{N} \right)^N \left( \frac{e q_B}{N} \right)^N = \left( \frac{e}{N} \right)^{2N} (q_A q_B)^N$$

$$= \left( \frac{e}{N} \right)^{2N} [q_A (q - q_A)]^N$$

$\Rightarrow$ has max. at $q_A = q/2$

$$\therefore \Omega_{\max} = \left( \frac{e}{N} \right)^{2N} \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^{2N}$$

***

how sharp is it ?
Let $q_A = \frac{q}{2} + x$, $q_B = \frac{q}{2} - x$.

$$\Omega = \left( \frac{e}{N} \right)^{2N} \left[ \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^2 - x^2 \right]^N \quad \text{When } x/q \ll 1$$

$$\ln \left[ \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^2 - x^2 \right]^N = N \ln \left[ \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^2 - x^2 \right]$$

$$= N \ln \left[ \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^2 \left( 1 - \frac{x^2}{(q/2)^2} \right) \right]$$

$$= N \left[ \ln \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^2 + \ln \left( 1 - \frac{x^2}{(q/2)^2} \right) \right]$$

$$x \ll q$$

$$\approx N \left[ 2 \ln \frac{q}{2} - \frac{x^2}{(q/2)^2} \right]$$

$$\therefore \Omega \approx \left( \frac{e}{N} \right)^{2N} \cdot \left( \frac{q}{2} \right)^{2N} e^{- N \frac{x^2}{(q/2)^2}}$$

$$= \Omega_{\max} \left\{ 
  \begin{array}{rl}  
    e^{- N (\frac{2x}{q})^2} & x = q_A - q/2 \\
    e^{- \frac{x^2}{(q/2\sqrt{N})^2}}
  \end{array}\right.$$

($\Omega$ vs $q_A$ 가우시안 곡선 다이어그램) : gaussian.

$\sigma \sim \frac{q}{\sqrt{N}}$ : increasingly sharper as $N$ grows.

***

Q. $N \ll q$

$\frac{q}{\sqrt{N}}$ is still large...
Can it be really sharp?
$\Rightarrow$ It's a matter of "scale".

($\Omega$ vs $q_A$ 다이어그램, 폭 $\sim \frac{q}{\sqrt{N}}$)
as $N \to \infty$

$$\frac{q/\sqrt{N}}{q} \to \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \to 0$$

Sharpness in stat. mech. (fractional)

$$\frac{\sigma_N}{\bar{X}_N} \xrightarrow{\text{as } N \to \infty} 0 \quad ; \text{relative fluctuation Vanishes when } N \to \infty .$$

In other words,
Let's say, we can measure $q^* = q/2$ with $1\%$ accuracy.

the fluctuation $q/\sqrt{N} \ll 1\% \text{ of } q^*$ as $N$ grows.

So you can't measure this !!!

## 2.5 The Ideal Gas (classical view.)

* Multiplicity of a monatomic ideal gas.

D a single molecule in an isolated system.

$\Omega_1 \propto V \cdot V_p$

$V$: volume in real space (multiplicity in position)

$V_p$: momentum space volume (multiplicity in momentum space)

What's "microstate" here ?

microstate $= (x, p)$

$$\therefore \Omega_1 \propto \int_{x^2+y^2+z^2 \le R^2} dx dy dz \int_{p_x^2+p_y^2+p_z^2 = 2mU} dp_x dp_y dp_z$$

energy conservation.

* What is the coefficient ?

$(x, p)$ - space : continuous $\leftarrow$ Heisenberg uncertainty principle

vs. 

$\Omega$: discrete i.e. it is not entirely continuous.

1D: $\Delta x \Delta p_x \approx h \leftarrow$ "one" microstate.

(상자 셀로 나누어진 $p_x$ vs $x$ 공간 다이어그램)

$$\therefore \Omega_1 = \frac{1}{h^3} \int_{x^2+y^2+z^2 \le R^2} dx dy dz \int_{p_x^2+p_y^2+p_z^2 = 2mU} dp_x dp_y dp_z$$
***

* Bohr - Sommerfeld quantization.

$$n h = \oint p \cdot dq$$

(위상 공간 셀 다이어그램) 

"one" quantum state in $(x, p)$ - space.

$$\Omega_1 = \frac{1}{h^3} V \cdot \frac{4}{3} \pi (2m U)^3$$

(2) two - particles.

$$\Omega_2 \propto \frac{1}{h^6} \int_{x_1^2+y_1^2+z_1^2 \le R^2} dx_1 dy_1 dz_1 \int_{x_2^2+y_2^2+z_2^2 \le R^2} dx_2 dy_2 dz_2 \int_{p_1x^2+p_1y^2+p_1z^2+p_2x^2+p_2y^2+p_2z^2 \le 2mU} dp_1x dp_1y dp_1z dp_2x dp_2y dp_2z$$

$$= \left[ \frac{1}{2} \right] \frac{V^2}{h^6} \times (\text{area of momentum hyperspace.})$$

$\rightarrow$ particles are indistinguishable.

(3) N particles.

$$\Omega_N = \frac{1}{N!} \frac{V^N}{h^{3N}} \times (\text{area of momentum hyperspace})$$

$$\left[ \int_{\sum_{i=1}^{3N} p_i^2 \le 2m U} \prod_{i=1}^{3N} d p_i \right] = \frac{2 \pi^{3N/2}}{(3N/2 - 1)!} ({2m U})^{(3N-1) / 2}$$

***

$$N \gg 1$$

$$\Omega_N \approx \frac{1}{N!} \frac{V^N}{h^{3N}} \frac{\pi^{3N/2}}{(3N/2)!} (\sqrt{2m U})^{3N}$$

One can write

$$\Omega(U, V, N) \equiv f(N) V^N U^{3N/2}$$

"a cost of" equipartition theorem.
$\Omega \propto U^{N f / 2} \quad f:$ \# of "quadratic" degree of freedom.

ideal gas. $p^2$: $3N \Rightarrow f=3N \Rightarrow \Omega \propto U^{3N/2}$

Einstein solid (3D) $x^2 + p^2$: $6N \Rightarrow f=6N \Rightarrow \Omega \propto U^{3N}$
$\leftarrow$ high-T (Classical)

* Interacting Ideal gases. (Thermally)

(두 상자 $U_A, V_A, N_A$와 $U_B, V_B, N_B$가 $\Delta U, \Delta V$를 교환하는 다이어그램)

$\Omega_{\text{total}} = [f(N)]^2 (V_A V_B)^N (U_A U_B)^{3N/2}$

In a similar way as in 2.4.

for E- exchange

$$\text{width} = \frac{U_{\text{total}}}{\sqrt{3N/2}} \quad \rightarrow \text{increasingly sharp as N grows.}$$

for V- adjust

$$\text{width} = \frac{V_{\text{total}}}{\sqrt{N}}$$

## 2.6. Entropy.

Multiplicity $\Omega$ tends to increase. $\leftarrow \rightarrow$ 2nd law.

Boltzmann:

$$S = k_B \ln \Omega \leftarrow \text{Entropy}$$

, additive. $[\Omega_{\text{total}} = \Omega_A \Omega_B \Rightarrow S_{\text{total}} = S_A + S_B]$

Ideal gas:

$$S = N k_B \left[ \ln \left( \frac{V}{N} \left( \frac{4\pi m U}{3 N h^2} \right)^{3/2} \right) + \frac{5}{2} \right]$$

... Sackur-Tetrode equation.

* free expansion: (adiabatic) (상자 다이어그램 $V_i \to V_f$)
$W=0$ (no pressure)
$\Rightarrow \Delta U = 0$

When U, N is fixed

$$\Delta S = N k_B \ln \frac{V_f}{V_i}$$

Entropy of Mixing.
(분리된 기체 A와 B 다이어그램, 각각 N 입자)

$\Delta S_A = k_B N \ln 2$

$\Delta S_B = k_B N \ln 2$

$S_{\text{total}} = \Delta S_A + \Delta S_B = 2 N k_B \ln 2$.

***

* Gibbs paradox

If gases are distinguishable.

$$S = N k_B \left[ \ln \left( V \left( \frac{4\pi m U}{3 N h^2} \right)^{3/2} \right) + \frac{3}{2} \right]$$

($gas_A$와 $gas_B$를 칸막이로 분리한 후 제거하는 다이어그램)

BUT. $\Delta S_{\text{total}} \ne \Delta S_A + \Delta S_B$.

(칸막이가 제거된 "same gas !!!" 다이어그램)

$\Rightarrow$ The gases are indistinguishable.
